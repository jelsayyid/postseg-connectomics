This work presents a conservative, modular post-segmentation pipeline for
automated split-error detection in connectomics data. The central research
question was: \emph{can a geometry-only, rule-based pipeline achieve high recall
on neural split detection without introducing excessive merge errors?}

On the CREMI Drosophila EM benchmark, the answer is clearly affirmative:
Precision = 1.000, Recall = 0.909, F1 = 0.952, with zero false positives. On
the XPRESS mouse white matter XNH challenge --- a harder domain where the
segmented structures are elongated tubular axons with interior splits --- the
pipeline achieves Recall = 0.994 on the training oracle and Recall = 0.885 on
the fully held-out validation volume, confirming generalization.

\paragraph{Key findings.}
Two innovations are responsible for the majority of the XPRESS recall improvement:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item The \textbf{skeleton-node graph} raises oracle coverage by 56.8
        percentage points over an endpoint-only graph in a single change, by
        enabling detection of interior splits along long axons.

  \item \textbf{Distance-conditioned scoring} prevents the proximity decay from
        suppressing long-range candidates whose alignment and continuity are
        strong, enabling the pipeline to accept splits at gaps up to 2000~nm.
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{Challenges.}
The dominant open challenge is precision: Precision $\approx 0.004$ on XPRESS,
meaning approximately 280 false-positive merge proposals for every true positive.
This stems from the geometric feature space being insufficient to discriminate
same-axon from cross-axon long-range pairs at the density of myelinated white
matter. A machine-learned filter (Experiment~20) improved precision $10\times$
(to 0.037) at the cost of recall, but could not maintain recall $\geq 0.99$.

A secondary challenge is the MinGapRule, which was intended to filter adjacency
false positives but also rejects 37 genuine short-gap splits, reducing training
recall to 0.966 and coverage to 75.9\%. Removing this rule restores the
Experiment~17 configuration (Recall = 0.994, Coverage = 83.5\%).

The coverage ceiling of 83.5\% is a structural limit of the 2000~nm search
radius: 247 oracle pairs have a gap or PCA-tip distance that exceeds this
threshold and cannot be reached by the current graph construction.

\paragraph{Summary of contributions.}
The pipeline is fully implemented, tested (428 tests, 0 failures), documented,
and open-source. It includes a YAML configuration system, six processing stages,
multi-format export, a ground-truth evaluation framework for two benchmarks,
an ML-based post-filter, and a visual diagnostic report generator. Twenty-three
experiments with detailed quantitative logs trace the development of each component.
